What learnings did you take from the chapter?

I loved this quote "What lies behind us and what lies ahead of us are tiny matters to what lies within us".
-Ralph Waldo Emerson
It's so true that we are only as compassionate and strong as we push ourselves to be, and this is a learned ability. I think the concept that we can teach ourselves to be more emotionally intelligence, and treat it like a 'practice' is great, as it gives us all something to work toward, rather than lament we wern't born a certain way. I also liked how the author links to the real benefits of EQ:
  • Stellar work performance
  • Outstanding leadership
  • The ability to create the conditions for happiness
Can't argue with that!
